seabed – solid surface of the earth that lies under the ocean, at least 1.8 kilometres underwater

natural resources – naturally existing things such as minerals, forests, coal and other energy sources that can be used by people

vertically – standing or pointing straight up and down at an angle 90 degrees, perpendicular to the ground, as opposed to horizontally parallel to the ground

the bends – decompression sickness - a serious medical sickness caused by nitrogen bubbles in the muscle tissues, resulting from return to the surface of the sea too quickly when diving

physiologically – relating to how the bodies of living humans and animals function

atmospheric pressure – the normal air pressure within the Earth’s atmosphere
