11.1. Present Continuous

1. The Present Continuous describes an activity happening now. She's wearing jeans. I'm studying English. 2. It also describes an activity in the near future. I'm playing tennis this afternoon. Jane's seeing her boyfriend tonight.

Spelling of verb + -ing

1. Most verbs just add -ing.
wear wearing
go going
cook cooking
hold holding
2. If the infinitive ends in -e, drop the -e.
write writing
smile smiling
take taking
3. When a one-syllable verb has one vowel and ends in a consonant, double the consonant.
sit sitting
get getting
run running

11.2. Present Simple and Present Continuous

1. The Present Simple describes things that are always true, or true for a long time. I come from Switzerland. He works in a bank. 2. The Present Continuous describes activities happening now, and temporary activities. Why are you wearing a suit? You usually wear jeans.
